Algunos autores se han aventurado a armar que el nombre de Canadá vienedel español, «aca nada»

In April 1534, James Cartier sailed from the port of St. Malo with twoships of 60 tons each, and 120 men. He arrived on the 10th of Mayfollowing on the coast of Newfoundland; and nding the countrycovered with ice and snow, sailed to the southward, and entered into a bay which bears at the present the name of Spanish Harbour,where he went on shore. It is reported, that the Spaniards had longbefore discovered this coast, but were in a hurry to go off again,crying out in their language Aca Nada, that is, there is nothing here;which words the Indians remembering, when the French came onshore, cried out also Aca Nada, Aca Nada! which the French took forthe name of the country so it has ever since called Canada. This is astrange derivation; but as we nd it in the best French authors, it maybe worth remarking (Owen; citado en Capa 13).
